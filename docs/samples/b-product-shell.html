Retirement isn’t a date — it’s math

By Know Plain Editorial · Updated Jul 2026 · Reviewed for accuracy

Most people pick a birthday and call it a plan. The better question is whether the math works — savings rate, withdrawal rate, and what happens if markets are ugly in the first decade.

Plain answer: Treat retirement as a funding problem. The date is an output, not an input.
