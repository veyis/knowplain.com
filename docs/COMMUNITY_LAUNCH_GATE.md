# Community launch gate

Know Plain must not promote open community posting until every gate below has a named owner and evidence from a two-week rehearsal.

## Required before promotion

- A named moderator checks the open report queue and pending submissions every business day.
- A backup moderator covers absences.
- Median pending-content review time is under 24 hours; the 95th percentile is under 72 hours.
- Privacy-exposure and credible-harm reports have a documented same-day escalation path.
- The report queue, moderation status changes, and rate-limit errors are observable without recording post bodies in analytics or error context.
- At least 20 useful, independently authored discussions exist; curated staff question hubs do not count as community participation.
- Fewer than 10% of reviewed submissions are spam or undisclosed promotion during the rehearsal.
- Guidelines, reporting, lock/hide behavior, and appeals/contact ownership have been manually tested.

## Until the gate passes

- Curated staff-written question hubs remain visually separate from open discussions.
- User-generated thread pages remain `noindex,follow`.
- New threads and replies default to `pending` and are visible only to their author and trusted moderation operations.
- Empty states describe the community honestly and direct readers to curated answers.
- No expert badge or credential label is shown for an unverified account.

Promotion is a product decision after the evidence above exists, not an automatic consequence of deploying the tables.
