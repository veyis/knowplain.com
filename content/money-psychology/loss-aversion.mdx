Imagine you find a $100 bill on the sidewalk. You feel a quick burst of joy. 

Now imagine you realize you dropped a $100 bill out of your pocket. The frustration and anger you feel will likely last much longer and be much more intense than the brief joy of finding the money.

This is **loss aversion**. Behavioral economists have found that psychologically, losses hurt roughly twice as much as equivalent gains feel good.

## Why it destroys wealth

In the stock market, loss aversion is incredibly dangerous. When a bear market hits and a portfolio drops 20%, the investor doesn't just feel a little bad—they feel an intense, overwhelming urge to *do something* to stop the pain.

The most common "solution" to this emotional pain is to sell everything and move to cash. 

The problem? Selling during a crash turns a temporary paper loss into a permanent realized loss. It guarantees that you will miss the eventual recovery, which often happens faster and more violently than people expect.

## How to protect yourself

1. **Stop checking:** During a bear market, checking your portfolio every day is like touching a hot stove repeatedly. Delete your brokerage app from your phone.
2. **Automate everything:** If your contributions are automated, you are forced to buy stocks when they are "on sale" during a crash, completely bypassing your emotional brain.
3. **Know your history:** Bear markets are a normal, expected part of investing. The stock market historically drops by 20% or more roughly once every 5 to 7 years. It is the price of admission for long-term growth.
