Retirement doesn't have to be a hard stop. A phased or part-time approach — sometimes called "barista FIRE" or a "victory lap" — is one of the most underrated levers in a plan, especially for a [late start](/topics/retirement/starting-retirement-savings-at-45). This spoke sits under the [Retirement hub](/topics/retirement).

## Why a little income does a lot

Part-time earnings punch above their weight because of *when* they land. Every dollar you earn in the first years of retirement is a dollar you **don't withdraw** from the portfolio — exactly when withdrawals do the most damage. That makes part-time work a powerful cushion against [sequence-of-returns risk](/topics/retirement/sequence-of-returns): even modest income lets your investments recover instead of being sold into a downturn.

## It also shrinks the funding gap

Working a few more years part-time does three things at once: it adds income, delays the start of full withdrawals, and can let you [delay Social Security](/topics/decision-tools/retirement-age-framework) for a larger guaranteed benefit. Combined, that can close a funding gap far faster than trying to save your way there.

## The Social Security earnings-test caveat

One thing to plan around: if you claim Social Security **before** your full retirement age *and* keep working, part of your benefit may be temporarily withheld under the **retirement earnings test** ([SSA](https://www.ssa.gov/benefits/retirement/planner/whileworking.html)). It isn't lost — it's repaid later as a higher benefit — but it's a reason many part-time retirees delay claiming while they're still earning.

## Key takeaways

- Early-retirement income cuts withdrawals when they're most dangerous.
- It adds income, delays withdrawals, and can let you delay Social Security.
- Watch the SSA earnings test if you claim before full retirement age and work.
